The People Mobility project develops computational methods and tools for understanding human movement patterns in urban environments.

By fusing anonymized GPS trajectory data, Wi-Fi probe request logs from public access points, transit smart card records, and geo-tagged social media posts, the platform constructs rich spatio-temporal models of how people move through cities.

## Analytical Capabilities

- Origin-destination matrix estimation
- Anomaly detection for unusual crowd gatherings
- Pedestrian flow simulation for event planning
- Accessibility gap analysis for public transit

## Applications

The system has been applied to study commuter patterns in the **Yogyakarta metropolitan area** and to model pedestrian evacuation dynamics around **Merapi volcano's** exclusion zone.

## Privacy

Privacy is ensured through differential privacy mechanisms applied at the data ingestion layer.
